Nobody tells this to people
  who are beginners, I wish
    someone told me. All of us
      who do creative work, we get
        into it because we have good taste.


               But there is this gap.


                                    For the first couple years you make
                                 stuff, it's just not that good. It's
                              trying to be good, it has potential,
                           but it's not. But your taste, the
                      thing that got you into
                   the game, is still killer. And your taste is why your work
                 disappoints you. A lot of people never get past this phase,
               they quit. Most people I know who do interesting, creative
            work went through years of this. We know our work doesn't
         have this special thing that we want it to have.

        We all go through this.

And if you are just starting out or you are still in this phase,
  you gotta know its normal and the most important thing you
    can do is do a lot of work. Put yourself on a deadline so that
      every week you will finish one story. It is only by going
        through a volume of work that you will close that gap, and
          your work will be as good as your ambitions. And I took
            longer to figure out how to do this than anyone I've ever met.

            It's gonna take awhile.

                  It's normal to take awhile.

                        You've just gotta fight your way through.
